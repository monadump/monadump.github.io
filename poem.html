A Poem

The Architecture of the Soul

A silent architect within the bone,

It builds its temple though it stands alone

Not made of matter, yet it shapes the clay,

A breath of forever in a single day.

The soul is neither compass nor the chart,

Yet navigates the ship without a part;

It is the question that the stars repeat,

The echo walking in its own heartbeat.

It wears the body like a borrowed coat,

Then hangs it on some quiet final note,

Yet does not cease, oh no, it does not cease

It merely trades the finite for the peace.

What is it, then? A light behind the eye?

A whisper that the mirror can't reply?

Perhaps the space between the thought and word,

The silence singing, though it can't be heard.

It tastes of love it never learned to name,

It carries joy like water, carries flame,

It knows the weight of every gentle touch,

And grieves far longer than the flesh grieves much.

So treat it well, this tenant of the rib,

This ancient child, this undiscovered fib

For you are not the house, you are the host,

The invited ghost that loves the living most.

And when the walls at last begin to fall,

Look up: the soul was never small at all.

It was the sky, pretending to be rain,

The infinite, learning to feel pain.
